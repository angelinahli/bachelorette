Season summary

The eleventh season of The Bachelorette features, for the first time ever, two Bachelorettes during the first episode. Kaitlyn Bristowe is a 29-year-old spin-class instructor[1] from Vancouver, British Columbia, and Britt Nilsson is a 27-year-old waitress from California. Both Bachelorettes were on the 19th season of The Bachelor, featuring Chris Soules. This season premiered on May 18, 2015 and concluded on July 27, 2015 in which 28-year-old personal trainer Shawn Booth was named the winner, the couple are living in Nashville, Tennessee.

Filming and development[edit]

Casting and contestants[edit]

Casting began during the tenth season of The Bachelorette. During the first episode, the Bachelors decide which of the ladies they would like to have as the Bachelorette for season 11.[2][3] In the second episode, which aired on May 19, 2015, it was revealed that Kaitlyn Bristowe was selected as the new Bachelorette.[4]

It began filming shortly after the finale of the nineteenth season of The Bachelor with the locale destinations including New York City, San Antonio and Ireland with appearances from Laila Ali, Amy Schumer, Doug E. Fresh and The Cranberries.[5]

Contestant Ryan McDill is the ex-boyfriend of Bachelor 18 winner Nikki Ferrell. It also includes season ten runner-up Nick Viall.

Future appearances[edit]

The Bachelor[edit]

Third place finalist Ben Higgins was chosen for the lead role on the twentieth season of The Bachelor. Nick Viall was chosen for the lead role on the twenty-first season of The Bachelor.

Bachelor in Paradise[edit]

Season 2

J.J. Lane III, Jonathan Holloway, Tanner Tolbert, Joshua Albers, Joe Bailey, Justin Reich, and Jared Haibon returned in the second season of Bachelor in Paradise. Neither Holloway, Albers, Lane, Bailey, nor Haibon found love. Tolbert left paradise engaged to Bachelor Nation alumna Jade Roper and Reich left Paradise in a relationship with Cassandra Ferguson.

Season 3

Haibon and Viall returned to compete in the third season of Bachelor in Paradise. Ryan Beckett also returned to compete in that season. None of them found love.

Season 4

Ben Zorn appeared in the fourth season in Bachelor in Paradise, but he quit in week three.

Dancing with the Stars[edit]

Outside of Bachelor Nation franchise, Viall competed for the twenty-fourth season of Dancing With the Stars on his 5th TV appearance partnering with Peta Murgatroyd.

The Bachelor Winter Games[edit]

Higgins appeared in his third Bachelor Nation appearance The Bachelor Winter Games as Team USA. He quit in week three.

Controversies[edit]

Snapchat spoiler[edit]

On June 12, weeks before the finale of the season, Bristowe posted a picture on Snapchat of her and Shawn Booth, her final choice, in bed together.[51]

Men Tell All slut-shaming[edit]

During the Men Tell All episode of the season, Chris Harrison read Bristowe a number of viewer tweets on air that referred to her as a "whore" and a "pathetic excuse for a human being" after she had sex with contestant Nick Viall earlier in the season before the fantasy suites.[52]
